🧳 My grandma, the explorer — by Nadia, 13

My grandma Rosa is seventy years old, and she has visited twenty-nine countries. Her suitcase is older than my dad, and it is covered in stickers from everywhere.

Last weekend she showed me her old photo albums. I learned something surprising: travelling used to be a completely different adventure.

🗺️ How she used to travel

Grandma didn't use to book hotels online — there was no internet! She used to write letters to hotels and wait three weeks for an answer.

She used to carry big paper maps, and she often got lost. "Getting lost was the best part," she says with a smile.

Photos were different too. She used to take one camera and two rolls of film — just seventy-two pictures for a whole month! Then she used to wait two weeks after the trip to see them. Some were blurry. She kept them anyway.

At the airport, she used to check in at a desk with a paper ticket. No apps, no QR codes, no phone.

And there used to be no cheap way to call home. She used to send postcards instead, and they often arrived AFTER she did. Imagine reading "Greetings from Rome!" while Grandma is already sitting in your kitchen.

Before every trip, she used to change her money into foreign currency and hide it in a secret pocket inside her jacket.

✈️ How I travel now

My family books everything on a phone in ten minutes. Our boarding passes live in an app next to my games.

When our flight had a two-hour delay last summer, we got a message immediately. Grandma just used to sit, wait and hope.

🎁 What hasn't changed

Some things are still the same. Grandma used to buy one small souvenir in every country, and I do exactly that too.

She used to ask local people for directions, and she taught me the magic words: "Excuse me, could you help me?"

One more thing hasn't changed: the night before a trip, she still can't sleep. "I used to be too excited to sleep at twenty," she laughs, "and I'm still too excited at seventy."

🌍 Our big plan

Next spring, we are going on a trip together — to Lisbon! Grandma will bring her paper map, and I will bring the app.

Between us, we will never get lost. Well… probably.

🔧 Grammar — Used To + Polite Questions 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer BEFORE you click it.

⏳ USED TO — past habits that STOPPED

used to + verb (only exists in the past!)

She used to carry paper maps. 🗺️
I didn't use to like flying.
Did you use to travel by ship?

🔑 After did/didn't → use to (no d!)

🙋 POLITE QUESTIONS — the traveller's key

Excuse me, could you tell me…

…where the gate is? ✅
…where is the gate? ❌

🔑 After "could you tell me", the words go back to statement order: where + the gate + is.
💡 The traveller's combo: "People used to ask strangers for directions. You can too: 'Excuse me, could you tell me where the station is?'"

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian says "régen" + normal past ("régen vonattal utaztunk") — English packs it into the verb: "we used to travel by train". And in polite questions, Hungarian keeps question order, but English flips back to statement order after "could you tell me…".
