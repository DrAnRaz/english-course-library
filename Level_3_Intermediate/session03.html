🦸 The great superpower survey — by Kofi, 14

Last month, our English club asked fifty students one big question: "If you could have one superpower, what would you choose — and what would you do with it?" The answers surprised us. Here are the results.

🥇 The winner: flying

Twenty students chose flying. Amara, twelve, gave the best reason: "If I could fly, I would never wait for the bus again. And I would visit my grandmother in Lagos every single weekend."

Nobody mentioned traffic. Flying kids never do.

🥈 Second place: invisibility

Fifteen students would like to be invisible. Most gave the same slightly scary reason: "If I were invisible, I would find out what my friends really say about me." Hmm — are you sure you want to know?

Our teacher laughed and hid her phone.

Priya, fourteen, was one of the fifteen, but her reason was kinder: "If I were invisible, I would visit the cinema for free — but I would feel lonely if nobody could see me." Her answer made the whole club go quiet for a moment.

🐱 The surprise: talking to animals

Ten students chose talking to animals. Bence, thirteen, explained: "If I could talk to my cat, I would finally understand why she knocks my pencils off the desk. I think she does it for fun."

⚡ And the rest

Five students picked super speed, mind reading or time travel. Only one person chose super strength. "Gyms are cheaper than superpowers," she wrote. Fair point.

One of the mind-reading fans added a warning: "If everyone could read minds, nobody would have any secrets — and school would be a very dangerous place." We gave that answer a special prize for wisdom.

🌟 What happens next

Then we asked a second question: "Forget magic — what will you do this year?" Suddenly the answers got real.

"If I practise every day, I will make the basketball team." "If I save my pocket money, I will buy a telescope." "If I get nervous before the test, I will breathe and count to ten."

My own answer? If I pass my exams in June, I will start a podcast about our club. And if I had more free time, I would interview every student in the school. One question is never enough.

Maybe that is the real lesson of our survey. Dreams need would. Plans need will. And the happiest people we know use both.

🔧 Grammar — First & Second Conditional 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says "real or dream?" out loud BEFORE you click the answer.

✅ FIRST CONDITIONAL — "real plans"

If + present simple, will + verb
Possible and real — this week, this year.

If I practise every day, I will make the team. 🏀
If it rains tomorrow, we will stay inside.

🔑 Think: a real chance

🌈 SECOND CONDITIONAL — "dreams"

If + past simple, would + verb
Imagined, unreal, magical — just pretend.

If I had wings, I would fly to school. 🕊️
If I were invisible, I would listen to everything.

🔑 Think: just imagine…
💡 The imagination switch: the past tense in "If I had wings" is NOT about the past — it's the switch that turns ON imagination. And yes, "If I were you…" is correct — dreams get special grammar!

💡 Hungarian tip: in Hungarian BOTH halves sound conditional — "ha lenNE, csinálNÁM". In English only the second half gets would; the if-part uses simple past. Never say "If I would have…"!
