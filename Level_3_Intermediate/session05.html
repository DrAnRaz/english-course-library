How to play:

1. Your teacher says ONE silly sentence, for example: "My cat can dance salsa."
2. You report it back: "You said that your cat could dance salsa!" 🕵️
3. Swap roles! You say a silly sentence about yourself — your teacher reports it. Catch every mistake!

No score here — just wake up your reporter brain! 🧠

Teacher note — Step 2

Keep sentences short and visual. Model the backshift with your voice: stress CAN → COULD, AM → WAS. Play 3–4 rounds each. If the student just repeats the exact words, ask: "Can you start with YOU SAID…?"

🐹 The Great Hamster Rumor

On Monday morning, Priya told her friend Marcus a small piece of news. She said that Biscuit, the class hamster, was a little bit fat. That was all. A tiny, boring fact.

📞 The message starts to change

On Tuesday, Marcus told Lena about it. But he didn't remember Priya's exact words. He said that Biscuit was too fat to run in his wheel. Lena was shocked.

On Wednesday, Lena told Tomás that the hamster couldn't move at all. Tomás was worried. He told his whole football team that Biscuit was the fattest hamster in the country.

🔥 Friday: total chaos

By Friday, everyone "knew" the story. Aisha announced that a TV crew was coming to film Biscuit. Somebody said that the head teacher had called a special hamster doctor from London. One little kid even asked if Biscuit would get his own YouTube channel.

🕵️ The truth comes out

Finally, their teacher, Mr Okafor, lifted Biscuit out of his cage. "Look," he said calmly. "Biscuit is fine. He is just very fluffy." The classroom went silent.

Then Priya admitted that she had started everything. "I only said that he was a LITTLE bit fat," she explained. "I never said anything about TV!"

Mr Okafor smiled and warned the class: "Be careful, reporters. Words change when they travel. If you report someone's words, report them honestly." Everybody looked at the floor. Biscuit ate a sunflower seed. He didn't care at all.

The next Monday, the class made a new rule: check the facts first, then speak. Priya wrote it on a big poster — and yes, she asked Biscuit for his opinion.

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: that Biscuit was a little bit fat · Tomás · that a TV crew was coming · he was just very fluffy. Open 1 guide: nobody remembered the exact words / each person changed the message a little. Open 2: check backshift + say/tell choice. The text is packed with reported speech models — before Step 5, point at "She said that Biscuit WAS fat" and ask: "Priya spoke on Monday about NOW — so why WAS?" That question is the bridge to the grammar.
🔧 Grammar — Reported Speech 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer BEFORE you click it.

🗨️ DIRECT SPEECH — the exact words

Quotation marks, word for word.

"I am tired," said Ana.
"I can swim," said Ben.

🔑 Looks like: " … "

📡 REPORTED SPEECH — one step back

said (that) + the verb moves back:
am/is → was · are → were
can → could · will → would
like → liked (present → past)

Ana said (that) she was tired.
Ben said he could swim.
🔑 Three magic moves: 1️⃣ the verb goes ONE step back in time · 2️⃣ I/you → he/she/they · 3️⃣ say has no person, tell NEEDS one:
She said that… ✅ · She told me that… ✅ · She said me… ❌

💡 Hungarian tip: in Hungarian the tense stays the same — "Azt mondta, hogy fáradt" (present!). English pulls the verb back: She said she WAS tired. When you report, take one step into the past.

✏️ Practice — tap the correct form

Teacher note — Step 5

If the student gets 6+/8, speed through and bank time for Step 6. Most common error = keeping the present ("She said she IS…"). Fix it with a gesture: point behind your shoulder = one step back. Item 4 vs 5 isolates say/tell — if missed, drill 3 quick oral swaps.

🤸 MOVE! — 1 minute break

Stand up! Air-write the word SAID in giant letters with your whole arm, then TOLD. Finish with 5 chair squats, counting down from five in English. Teacher does it too! 😄
