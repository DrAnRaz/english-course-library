How to play:

1. Your teacher starts miming an action on camera (typing, swimming, cooking…). 🎭

3. The teacher answers: "I was…" — then you swap! You mime, the teacher freezes you and guesses: "You were dancing!"

No score here — just wake up your English brain! 🧠

🎬 The night everything went dark — by Nadia, 13

Everyone remembers one perfect family evening. Mine happened three weeks ago, and — funny thing — it started with a total disaster. This is the true story.

🍿 A normal Saturday

Last Saturday began like every Saturday. My little brother Samir was building a tower of cushions, and our dog Biscuit was sleeping on my feet. Dad was making popcorn, and Mum and I were choosing a film.

💥 Then it happened

At exactly eight o'clock, we were watching the first scene when — CLICK. Every light in the house went out. The TV died, the fridge stopped humming, and the whole street turned black.

😱 A little bit of panic

Samir screamed and dropped a cushion on the dog. Biscuit was barking at the darkness like a tiny hero.

I was looking for my phone when I tripped over the sofa and sat on the popcorn bowl. My heart was racing.

Mum was calling our names in the dark. "Is everyone OK?" she asked. I said yes — but there was popcorn in my hair, in my socks, everywhere.

🔦 The investigation

Dad found a torch, and we walked around the house like detectives. Strange shadows were dancing on the walls. Nothing was broken — the storm outside had killed the power.

While we were checking the garden, our neighbour Mr. Okafor called over the fence: "Is your power off too? The whole street is dark!"

Up and down the street, torch lights were flashing in windows like tiny stars. Somewhere a car alarm was beeping, and two cats were crying on a wall. The night felt strange and exciting at the same time.

🕯️ The best part

So we did something we never do. Mum lit candles, and we were telling scary stories in the dark when, suddenly, the lights came back on.

Dad's story was the scariest — he was describing a ghost train when Biscuit howled, and we all jumped. And do you know what Samir shouted when the lights returned? "Turn them off again!" Now we have a new family rule: one Saturday every month, the lights go off on purpose. No TV. Just stories.

Last week we had our first "dark Saturday". While Dad was telling his ghost-train story again, Biscuit fell asleep in the middle of it. Samir says that next time HE is telling the scariest story. I can't wait.

🔧 Grammar — Past Continuous vs Past Simple 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer BEFORE you click it.

🎥 PAST CONTINUOUS — "the background film"

was/were + verb-ing
The long action — it was already happening. The camera is rolling.

We were watching a film. 🍿
Biscuit was sleeping on my feet.

🔑 Signal word: while

💥 PAST SIMPLE — "the interruption"

verb + -ed / irregular
The short, sudden action that breaks in. CLICK! BANG!

The lights went out.
Samir screamed.

🔑 Signal word: when
💡 The magic pattern: "I was doing X when Y happened." — or flip it: "While I was doing X, Y happened."
"I was looking for my phone when I tripped over the sofa."

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian has ONE past tense — the little word "éppen" does the job of past continuous: "éppen tévét néztem" = "I was watching TV". Ask yourself: long background action (continuous) or sudden moment (simple)?

Build your own "…when suddenly!" story — true or invented. First pick your ingredients, then film it in words. 🎬

First, tap 2–3 story ingredients:

🎥 Scene 1 — set the camera (2 background actions, past continuous)
💥 Scene 2 — the interruption (past simple: what suddenly happened?)
🏁 Scene 3 — the ending (how did it finish? how did you feel?)
🗣️ Now tell it out loud! Read your story to your teacher like a film trailer voice. Ready for the question: "What were you doing when it happened?"
⏭ SKIP-IF-SHORT: do only Scene 1 + the spoken story; type Scenes 2–3 as homework.

Teacher note — Step 6

Push for the two-tense pattern in speech: at least TWO "was/were + -ing" in Scene 1 and ONE clean past simple interruption in Scene 2. Follow-ups that force the tenses: "What was the weather doing? Who was with you? And then what happened?"
