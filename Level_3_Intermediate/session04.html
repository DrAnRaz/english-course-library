🍫 The secret journey of a chocolate bar

Look at a chocolate bar. It seems so simple. But that little bar is touched by thousands of hands around the world — and its journey is longer than most holidays.

🌱 It starts on a tree

Cocoa beans are grown in hot countries like Ghana and Ecuador. The heavy pods are cut from the trees by hand, because machines damage them. Inside each pod, about forty beans are hidden in sweet white pulp.

🚢 The long trip

The beans are dried in the sun for about a week. Then they are packed into big sacks and shipped across the ocean. Your chocolate was probably carried by ship, train AND truck before it reached the factory.

At the port, the sacks are checked and weighed. One sack holds about sixty kilograms of beans — enough for thousands of bars.

🏭 Inside the factory

At the factory, the beans are roasted, crushed and mixed with sugar and milk. The warm liquid chocolate is poured into moulds and cooled.

Finally, every bar is wrapped by fast machines — about six hundred bars every minute! The smell in a chocolate factory is famous. Workers say you stop noticing it after a month — imagine that.

Quality is checked at every step. Broken bars are removed from the line, and — sad news — they are not given to visitors. They are melted and used again. Nothing is wasted in a good factory.

🕰️ A little history

Chocolate was not always a bar. For centuries it was drunk, not eaten — the Maya people made it into a spicy, bitter drink.

The first modern chocolate bar was created in 1847, in England. Milk chocolate was invented about thirty years later, in Switzerland.

Today, millions of tonnes of chocolate are eaten every year around the world. New flavours are invented all the time — white, dark, even chocolate with chilli. But the beans still come from the same hot countries where the story begins.

🤔 Think about it

So who really "makes" chocolate? Farmers, sailors, factory workers, inventors. One simple bar is created by a whole planet working together.

Next time a chocolate bar is opened — by you! — remember its journey. It was grown on a farm, dried in the sun, carried across an ocean and shaped in a factory. Not bad for something that disappears in two minutes.

🔧 Grammar — The Passive Voice 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer BEFORE you click it.

🏭 PRESENT PASSIVE — "how it works today"

am/is/are + past participle
Facts, processes, how things happen now.

Cocoa is grown in Ghana. 🌱
The bars are wrapped by machines.

🔑 is/are + 3rd form (made, grown, sold…)

🕰️ PAST PASSIVE — "the history"

was/were + past participle
Finished events — often with a year.

The first bar was created in 1847.
Milk chocolate was invented in Switzerland.

🔑 was/were + 3rd form
💡 The magic move: the OBJECT jumps to the front when the action matters more than the doer.
Farmers grow cocoa. → Cocoa is grown (by farmers).
Add "by…" only if the doer is interesting!

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian usually says it with "they" — "Ghánában termesztik" (they grow it in Ghana). English flips it: "It is grown in Ghana." Ask yourself: do I know WHO did it? If not (or it doesn't matter) → passive!
