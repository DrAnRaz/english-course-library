🕵️ The Case of the Vanishing Cookies

Detective Nadia Flores, age twelve, got up at seven o'clock on Saturday. She put on her lucky coat and turned on her computer. There was a new message: "HELP! Our cookies keep disappearing! — The Reyes family."

🍪 The crime scene

Nadia arrived at the Reyes house at nine. Mrs Reyes explained the mystery. Every night she baked cookies. Every morning, three or four were gone. "We have almost run out of chocolate chips," she said sadly.

Nadia looked for clues. She found crumbs on the floor and a small, muddy footprint near the window. She also found a long grey hair. It was not human hair. It was not cat hair either. Hmm.

She picked up the hair with her tweezers and put it in a little evidence bag. "A good detective keeps everything," she said. Then she checked the kitchen window. It didn't close properly — anyone small could open it from outside.

🌙 The stake-out

That night, Nadia hid behind the sofa. She turned off all the lights and waited. She wanted to find out the truth. At midnight, she heard a soft sound. She almost gave up because she was so sleepy — but then the kitchen door opened!

To stay awake, she read her detective handbook under a blanket, with a torch. Chapter one says: "Never give up before midnight." Chapter two says: "Bring snacks." Nadia forgot the snacks, of course.

🐾 Case closed

A fat raccoon climbed in, picked up two cookies with its little hands, and hurried away. Nadia laughed so hard she fell over. The next day she told the family everything.

Now Mr Reyes closes the window at night, and the raccoon hangs out in the garden instead — where the family leaves it a small, healthy snack. Nadia took off her lucky coat, went home, and wrote in her notebook: "Case number nine: closed. The cookie thief had a tail."

Mrs Reyes was so happy that she baked a special thank-you batch. "You never gave up," she said. "You are welcome here any time." Nadia wrote the date in her notebook, next to the grey hair in its little bag.

P.S. Nadia's little brother now wants her to look after his hamster. Detectives never rest.

🔧 Grammar — How Phrasal Verbs Work 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer BEFORE you click it.

🧩 VERB + PARTICLE = NEW MEANING

give 🎁 + up ⬆️ = give up = stop trying 🏳️

You often CANNOT guess the meaning from the parts. Learn the whole chunk, like one new word.

look for = try to find 🔍
look after = take care of 🐶
Same verb, different little word — totally different meaning!

✂️ SOME SPLIT, SOME DON'T

Many can split:
turn on the light = turn the light on ✅

With it / them, the little word goes LAST:
turn it on ✅ · turn on it ❌

Some NEVER split:
look after the dog ✅ · look the dog after ❌
🔑 Detective habit: when you meet a new phrasal verb, write the WHOLE chunk plus one example sentence in your notebook. Chunks, not pieces!

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian igekötők work in a similar way — FELkel = get up, KIderít = find out, FELad = give up. The big difference: the English little word comes AFTER the verb, not before it.
