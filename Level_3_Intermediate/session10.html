📖 Reading — The Mystery of Room 9 10 minStep 4
📹 Zoom: share screen. Student reads one chunk aloud, you read the next — take turns. Then they annotate answers on your screen (Zoom → Annotate).

Read the story of Milo's escape. Tap 🔊 to hear any part read aloud.

🔐 The strange invitation

On Friday afternoon, Milo found a strange envelope in his schoolbag. Inside was a golden ticket.

It said: "You have been chosen. Come to the old library at four o'clock. Bring your English brain."

🚪 The locked room

Milo had never seen the library so quiet. At exactly four o'clock, the heavy door closed behind him — click!

He looked around: dark shelves, a giant clock, and nine locked boxes on a long wooden table.

A friendly voice spoke from a hidden speaker: "Welcome to Room 9. You have one hour. If you solve all the puzzles, you will find the key."

🕰️ The first clue

Milo was looking at the bookshelf when he noticed something odd. One green book was upside down.

While he was pulling it out, a secret drawer opened in the old desk. Inside lay a yellow note.

The note said: "This library was built in 1891. The first puzzle was hidden by the builder himself."

🗝️ The riddle

Under the desk, Milo found a small radio. The voice told him that the answer was on the ceiling.

He looked up and laughed. Many years ago, someone had painted a giant golden seven above the lamp.

Seven turned the lock. Then came a word puzzle, a number code, and a map that was drawn in invisible ink.

🎉 The escape

At five to five, Milo turned the final key. The door swung open, and his teacher was waiting outside with a huge smile.

"Congratulations! You have used everything you learned this year. If you keep practising, you will be unstoppable."

Milo grinned and asked: "Can I do it again?"

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: a golden ticket · pulling out a book · in 1891 · that the answer was on the ceiling. Open Q1 accepts any of: "This library was built in 1891." / "The first puzzle was hidden by the builder himself." / "a map that was drawn in invisible ink." / "You have been chosen." Open Q2 needs "If I … I would…". The bold phrases are a tour of the whole level — after the quiz, ask the student to NAME the grammar of two bold phrases ("that's passive!", "that's past continuous!").
