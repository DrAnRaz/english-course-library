🧪 The experiment — by Tomas, 14

Last month our teacher gave us a challenge: seven days with no social media. Most of my class said "no way". I said yes — and now I'm glad I did.

📱 My old habits

Before the challenge, I spent too much time on my phone. I watched a lot of videos, I checked every notification, and I streamed two episodes every night.

My favourite influencer posted something new every hour, and I watched every advertisement before every video. My eyes were tired, my homework was late, and my dog was bored.

My family noticed too. At dinner I answered every question with "hmm", because I was reading comments under the table. My sister said I loved my phone more than my dog. That hurt — mostly because it was a little bit true.

How many hours a day? About five. That is not a little — that is a lot!

😖 Day one was hard

The first day was horrible. I picked up my phone about fifty times, but there was nothing to check. I had too much free time and too few ideas.

Day two was not much better. I found my old handball in the garden, threw it at the wall about a hundred times, and learned an important fact: without a phone, an afternoon has a lot of hours in it.

😊 The surprise

By day four, something changed. I read a few chapters of a real book, and I cooked dinner with my mum.

I even took my dog outside twice a day. Suddenly I had enough time for everything, and I slept much better.

On day six, my friend Bence called me — with his voice, not a message! We talked for an hour.

Funny detail: at first we didn't have much to say, because usually we just send each other memes. After ten minutes, we had too many topics and not enough time.

🤔 What I learned

Do I hate my phone now? No! I still stream series, and I listen to a podcast on the school bus.

But now I follow one simple rule: a little screen time is fine, but too much steals your whole day.

I turned off most notifications, and I check social media only twice a day. My advice? Try one screen-free evening. You have nothing to lose — except a few silly videos.

🔧 Grammar — Quantifiers: How Much Is Too Much? 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer BEFORE you click it.

🔢 COUNTABLE — you can count them

videos, followers, podcasts, ads, hours

How many videos?
a few videos · too many ads · too few ideas

🔑 many · a few · too many ↔ too few · not many

🌊 UNCOUNTABLE — no plural, no counting

time, news, information, music, homework

How much time?
a little time · too much news

🔑 much · a little · too much · not much
💡 The team players: a lot of and enough work with BOTH.
"I watch a lot of videos. I don't have enough time."

💡 Hungarian tip: in Hungarian, "sok" works for everything — English splits it into many (countable) and much (uncountable). And careful: news, information, homework, time are UNCOUNTABLE in English, even though they feel countable in Hungarian!

Require at least 4 different quantifiers in the spoken version (a lot of, too much/many, a few/a little, enough). Follow-ups that force quantifiers: "How many channels? How much time? Is that enough sleep?" If they invent a funny rule, celebrate it — memorable rules stick.
