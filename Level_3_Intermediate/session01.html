🌟 My bucket list update — by Maya, 13

Everyone has a bucket list — a list of things you want to do in your life. I started mine when I was ten. Three years later, here's my update!

✅ Things I have done

I have ridden a roller coaster — actually, I've ridden seven! The first time was in 2023, at a theme park in Vienna. I screamed so loudly that my dad laughed for ten minutes.

I have acted on stage twice. Last spring I played a talking cat in our school play. I forgot one line, but nobody noticed because I meowed instead.

I have also won a medal. It was a swimming competition last summer. I didn't win gold — I came third — but I have never felt so proud.

Oh, and I have made three videos for my cousin's channel. We filmed the last one in our garden, and a bird flew straight into the shot. We kept it in the video — everyone said it was the funniest part!

❌ Things I haven't done — yet!

I haven't travelled outside Europe yet, and I have never tried sushi. My best friend Zoe says raw fish is delicious. I'm not so sure!

I haven't met a celebrity either, but I have seen one! Two years ago, a famous singer walked past our café. I wanted to say hello, but I was too shy. I just stood there holding my hot chocolate.

My next goal? I want to learn the guitar. I have already chosen the song I want to play first. Wish me luck!

🔧 Grammar — Present Perfect vs Past Simple 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer BEFORE you click it.

✅ PRESENT PERFECT — "the experience"

have/has + past participle
WHEN doesn't matter — it's about your life "so far".

I have ridden a roller coaster. 🎢
She has never tried sushi.

🔑 Signal words: ever, never, just, already, yet

⏰ PAST SIMPLE — "the story"

verb + -ed / irregular
WHEN matters — a finished moment.

The first time was in 2023.
I screamed so loudly!

🔑 Signal words: yesterday, last year, in 2023, when I was ten
💡 The magic pattern: open the topic with present perfect, then tell the story with past simple.
"I have won a medal. It was a swimming competition. I came third."

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian has ONE past tense, so this is new thinking, not translation. Ask: "Am I talking about my life in general (perfect), or one finished moment (simple)?"
