⚡ Warm-Up — Finger Flash 5 minStep 2
📹 Zoom: cameras on, no screen share. Hands close to the camera!

How to play:

1. Teacher shows fingers. ✋
2. You shout the number! "Five!"
3. Now YOU show fingers. Teacher shouts.
4. Speed round! Faster, faster! 😄

No score here. Just count and shout! 🧠

Teacher note — Step 2

TPR counting. Start 1–5, then mix up to 10, then two hands flashed twice for 11+ (recognition). Sneak in "How many?" as a chunk. Wrong shouts are funny, not failures — laugh together and re-flash.

Quiz answers: nine · ten · three · a hamster 🐹. Open 1: "I am [age] years old." — full chunk, number word or digit both fine. Open 2: "six apples" (or "6 apples"). Echo-read line by line; stop at every number and count the emoji together with fingers up.
🔧 Patterns — Age Talk 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student SAYS the answer before you click it.

🎂 THE AGE QUESTION

One question. One answer. Always the same!

"How old are you?" 🤔
"I am nine years old." 😊

🔑 Pattern: I am + number + years old.

🔢 BIG NUMBERS: -TEEN

13 to 19 end in -teen!

13 thirteen · 14 fourteen
15 fifteen · 16 sixteen
17 seventeen · 18 eighteen · 19 nineteen
20 = twenty 🎉
💡 Magic mini-talk — say it 3 times, faster each time:
"How old are you?" — "I am ten years old. And you?"

💡 Hungarian tip: "9 éves vagyok" → "I am 9 years old." Use am, never "have". And don't forget "years old"!

✏️ Practice — tap the right answer

Teacher note — Step 5

Chunks, not rules. Drill the student's REAL age chunk until automatic, then ask about family: "How old is your mum?" (any honest guess is fine — the chunk is the point). The -teen box is recognition only; production comes at L1.

🤸 MOVE! — 1 minute break

Stand up! Do 10 small jumps. 🦘 Count out loud: one, two, three… ten! Then stretch up high and count down: five, four, three, two, one!

The room-count is the fun part: send the student hunting ("Find the windows! Count them in English!"). Dictation mode OK. Model the answer shape first: "Two windows!" Accept single words + number; celebrate full sentences extra loud.
