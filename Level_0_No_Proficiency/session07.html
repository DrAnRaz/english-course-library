How to play:

2. You act the animal! Move! 🤸

No score here. Just play and move! 🧠

Teacher note — Step 2

Use sounds the student may know from home (dog, cat, cow). Model acting BIG — flap arms, gallop. If the student names an animal in their own language, echo it in English: "Yes! A cow! Moo!" This is pure TPR — comprehension before production.

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: Woof! · pink · big · the bird. Open questions — any animal is correct; accept single words ("dog", "Woof!"). Model the full line back: "My favorite animal is the dog. Woof!" (same frame as S2's "My favorite letter is…"). Read line → student echoes line. Keep a steady rhythm; the emoji carries the meaning.
🔧 Magic Pattern — "It's a ___" 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. Student SAYS each answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

🗣️ THE MAGIC PATTERN

Point at an animal. Say:

🐶 It's a dog.
🐱 It's a cat.
🐮 It's a cow.

🔑 Same start every time: It's a…

🎵 ANIMAL TALK

Animals "say" sounds:

The dog says woof!
The cat says meow!
The duck says quack!
💡 Say it like one word: "Itsa dog!" Fast and happy! 🎉

💡 Hungarian tip: English animals talk differently! Hungarian dog: "vau-vau". English dog: "woof-woof"! Funny, right? 😄

✏️ Practice — tap the correct one

Teacher note — Step 5

This is a PATTERN, not a rule — never explain "it is" contraction. Drill orally as a chant: you point at an emoji anywhere on screen, student fires "It's a ___!" If the quiz goes 6+/8, add speed rounds; if under 5, go back to choral repetition with the 🔊 buttons.
