Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: red · yellow · blue · my bag. Open questions — any school things count; single words OK ("pen, book"). Echo the full chunk back: "This is my pen!" Have the student HOLD each named object while reading its line — object + word + sound together.
🔧 Magic Pattern — "This is my ___" 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. Student SAYS each answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

🗣️ THE MAGIC PATTERN

Hold a thing. Say:

🎒 This is my bag.
🖊️ This is my pen.
📖 This is my book.

🔑 Same start: This is my…

🎒 WHERE IS IT?

Things go IN the bag:

The book is in my bag.
The pen is in my bag.

🔑 Put it in! Say: in my bag
✂️ Mini-pattern: one thing → This is; many things → These are!
"This is my pen." 🖊️ (one) · "These are my scissors." ✂️ · "These are my crayons." 🖍️🖍️🖍️ (many)
💡 Say it and DO it: hold the thing up — "This is my pen!" Drop it in — "In my bag!" 🎒

💡 Hungarian tip: English "my" comes FIRST: my bag. Hungarian puts it at the end: "táskám". Say "my" first! 👉

✏️ Practice — tap the correct one

Teacher note — Step 5

Pattern, not grammar — never explain "this/these" difference beyond one aside on scissors ("two parts — THESE"). Physical drill: student packs real objects into the real bag, one sentence per object. 6+/8 → speed pack; under 5 → choral chant with 🔊 and real objects.
