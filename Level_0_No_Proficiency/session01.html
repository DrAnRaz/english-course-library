🎯 Hello & Today's Goal 2 minStep 1
📹 Zoom: cameras on. Big smile, big wave! Teacher reads the goal with 🔊 — student copies it back.

🎯 Today: two magic lines — "Hello! I'm ___" + "How are you?"

Teacher note — Step 1

Say: "Today you learn two magic lines: 'Hello! I'm ___.' and 'How are you?' Say them after the sound. You can do it!"
This may be the student's FIRST English lesson ever. Speak slowly, gesture everything, smile a lot. Every phrase: 🔊 first, then choral repeat ×2, then solo. The student's own language is fine for comfort — always loop back to the English chunk. Do NOT explain grammar; these are memorized chunks.
⚡ Warm-Up — The Wave Game 5 minStep 2
📹 Zoom: cameras on, no screen share. Copy-me game — student mirrors your wave and voice.

How to play:

1. Teacher waves. Teacher says "Hello!" 👋
2. You wave. You say "Hello!" too.
3. Now silly voices! 🤖 Robot hello. 🤫 Whisper hello. 🦖 GIANT hello!
4. Last one: fastest wave wins! 😄

No score here. Just play and speak! 🧠

Teacher note — Step 2

Pure TPR + fearless noise-making. Sequence: normal → robot → whisper → giant → opera singer. Student copies gesture + voice each time. Finish with "Hello, [name]!" and elicit "Hello, teacher!" back. Accuracy doesn't matter yet; volume and courage do.

Teacher note — Step 3

Drill: 🔊 → choral ×2 → solo, with a gesture for every card (wave = hello, moon hands = good night, thumbs up = I'm fine). Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: Pip · Nia · I'm fine 👍 · Good night 🌙. Open 1: any "Hi! I'm [student's name]." Open 2: "I'm fine, thank you!" or "I'm happy!" — the chunk counts, not spelling. Read each line with 🔊, student echoes. Emojis carry the meaning: point FIRST, speak SECOND. Typing help is fine — student can dictate, you type.

💬 ASKING & ANSWERING

One question. One answer. Always the same!

"How are you?" 🤔
"I'm fine, thank you!" 👍
"I'm happy!" 😄

🔑 Question → answer. Like a song!
💡 Magic mini-talk — say it 3 times, faster each time:
"Hello!" — "Hi!" — "How are you?" — "I'm fine, thank you!"

💡 Hungarian tip: "How are you?" = "Hogy vagy?" — the answer always starts with "I'm…". Learn it as ONE chunk, like a song line.

✏️ Practice — tap the right answer

Teacher note — Step 5

Drill CHUNKS, not single words — no grammar talk at L0. If the student is stuck, you say the first half ("I'm…") and they finish ("…fine, thank you!"). 6+/8 = great, move on. Under 5 = redo the wrong ones aloud with gestures (wave = hello, moon hands = good night).
