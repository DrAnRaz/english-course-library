Teacher note — Step 1

Homework check (S4): 3 shapes drawn + "It's a ___ ___." under each — ask the student to show the picture and read the lines aloud; listen for color-before-shape order.

Say the goal slowly, twice; student echoes. Preview: point at yourself, say "me!", then show a family photo (or draw stick figures) and say "my family". Keep every instruction under 8 words, gesture everything.
⚡ Warm-Up — Finger Family! 5 minStep 2
📹 Zoom: cameras on, no screen share. Hands up close to the camera!

How to play:

1. Show your hand. 🖐️
2. Teacher says: "daddy finger!" 👨
3. Wiggle that finger! 🤸
4. Say the word back! 🗣️

Thumb = daddy 👨 · pointer = mummy 👩 · middle = brother 👦 · ring = sister 👧 · pinky = baby 👶

Teacher note — Step 2

Classic Finger Family. Sing it if the student enjoys singing ("Daddy finger, daddy finger, where are you?") — melody carries vocabulary at L0. Do both hands, speed up for laughs. No score, no correction.

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: sister · cookies · a dog · his family. Open questions — sample answers: 1) "my mum, my dad, my brother" 2) "I love my grandma." Single words are a win; echo back the full chunk. Sensitivity: family setups differ — if a word is painful, swap freely (aunt, cousin, dog all count as family here).
🔧 Magic Pattern — "This is my ___" 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. Student SAYS the answer first. Then teacher taps it.

✅ MAGIC PATTERN 1 — show someone

This is my + person.

This is my mum. 👩
This is my dad. 👨
This is my grandma. 👵

❤️ MAGIC PATTERN 2 — love someone

I love my + person.

I love my sister. 👧
I love my family. 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

💡 Point and say: point at a photo or draw your family. 👉 Then say: "This is my… mum!" One point, one sentence.

💡 Hungarian tip: English always needs MY: "my mum" = „anyukám". One Hungarian word = two English words. Never forget the "my"! 😊

Teacher note — Step 6

The show-and-point moment is the real output; typing is secondary. Sample answers: "This is my mum." ×3 + "I love my family." Pets count as family — a dog line is a joy, allow it. Ask "Who is this?" and let them answer with the chunk.
