Text 1 · easy

Text 2 · medium

Lena has just come back from her first summer camp, and she can't stop talking about it. She was nervous at the beginning because she didn't know anyone.

But on the second day, while everyone was building a raft, she made two new friends. If the camp runs again next year, she will definitely go back.

Text 3 · hard

It is often claimed that teenagers are addicted to their phones, yet the evidence is more nuanced than the headlines suggest. Had researchers relied on screen-time alone, they would have missed what actually matters: not how long young people are online, but what they do there.

A quiet hour spent reading articles is hardly comparable to an hour of doom-scrolling, however similar the two may look on a usage chart.

🗣️ Part E — Speaking 5 minteacher-scored
📹 Zoom: cameras on, screen share OFF. Just talk.

Ask 2–3 of these, going as deep as the student can follow:

1️⃣ Easy: "Tell me about your family." / "What do you do after school?"
2️⃣ Medium: "Tell me about the best day you have ever had. What happened?"
3️⃣ Hard: "If you could change one rule at school, what would it be and why?"
4️⃣ Very hard: "Some people say video games are bad for kids. Do you agree? Convince me."

Now rate what you heard:

DimensionRate 0–6 (matches library levels)
Fluency — flow, pauses, keeps going without translating in head
Grammar range — tenses attempted & accuracy
Vocabulary — word variety, precision

Anchors: 0 = isolated words · 1 = memorized phrases ("I am 12") · 2 = simple sentences, frequent errors · 3 = connected speech, past/future attempted · 4 = comfortable opinions, complex sentences · 5 = nuanced, rare errors · 6 = near-native ease.

How the recommendation works

Every auto-scored item is tagged with a level (1–6). A level counts as "passed" when ≥60% of its items are correct. The recommendation = highest passed level such that all lower levels also passed (no gaps), averaged with your speaking rating (rounded down when in doubt — starting slightly low builds confidence; you can always jump a level after two easy sessions). Borderline (within half a level) → the note suggests trying the lower level first.
