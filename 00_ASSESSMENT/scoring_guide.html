🧭 Placement Scoring Guide (teacher only — don't share with students)

The 5-minute version

1. Open placement.html, share screen on Zoom, frame it as a game ("treasure map, not a test").
2. Parts A–D: student answers aloud, you click. Stop a ladder early after ~3 misses in a row — skipped items don't hurt the score.
3. Part E: 5 minutes of real conversation, rate Fluency / Grammar / Vocabulary 0–6.
4. Press 🧮 Calculate level → recommendation appears with per-level bars.
5. Type your observations, press 💾 Save report, keep the .txt in the student's folder.

Level anchors (what each level looks like live)

LevelCEFRYou will hear/see…
0 — No Proficiencypre-A1Isolated words at best; needs L1 support; can't form "I am 12" reliably.
1 — BeginnerA1Memorized chunks + simple present; understands slow, clear questions about self.
2 — ElementaryA2Simple sentences about routines/past events; frequent errors that don't block meaning.
3 — IntermediateB1Connected speech; attempts perfect/conditional; can tell a story and give simple opinions.
4 — Upper-Int.B2Comfortable debate; complex sentences; errors rare enough that you stop noticing them.
5 — ProficientC1Nuance, register shifts, idiomatic reach; self-corrects elegantly.
6 — Near-NativeC2Effortless; humor, style, cultural references; you forget it's a lesson.

Decision rules

• A level "passes" at ≥60% of its answered items; the auto level is the highest passed level with no gaps below it.
• Final = auto level averaged with your speaking rating, rounded down.
• Big gap (≥2 levels) between written and spoken? Start at the LOWER — the sessions stretch upward easily.
• Borderline? Start lower. Two easy sessions cost nothing; a too-hard first session can cost the student's confidence.
• Neurodivergent students: allow extra time, repeat audio twice, never rush the ladder. The score measures English, not processing speed.

💡 Re-test after every ~10 sessions, or sooner if the level's challenge rounds are consistently ⭐⭐⭐. Moving up is a celebration — say so out loud.
