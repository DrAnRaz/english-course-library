How to play:

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: eleven · a nurse · Bea · old and slow. Open-question key: 1. any person + "because I am + adjective" (accept any true adjective, require the full "I am" pattern); 2. serious and funny. Point at "He IS old" vs "We ARE happy" and ask: "Why is one word different?" — that question opens Step 5.
🔧 Grammar — am / is / are 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

In English you always need a little helper word — am, is, or are. It is the glue between the person and the description. 🔗

✅ THE THREE HELPERS

🟦 I → am
🟪 he / she / it → is
🟩 you / we / they → are

I am funny. 😄
She is kind. 💛
They are clever. 🧠

⚠️ NEVER DROP A WORD

Every sentence needs person + helper:

❌ I funny.
✅ I am funny.

❌ She kind.
✅ She is kind.
💡 The magic pattern: person + am/is/are + describing word.
"My dog is small. My friends are funny. I am happy."

💡 Hungarian tip: in Hungarian you can say "Vicces vagyok" with no extra word. In English you ALWAYS need both parts: "I am funny." Never drop the little helper!

Teacher note — Step 5

If the student gets 6+/8, speed up and bank time for Step 6. If under 5, redo the wrong ones aloud with the pointing trick: point at yourself (am), at one imaginary person (is), at both of you (are).

Push for FULL sentences ("My name is…", not just the name). Ask 2–3 "Are you…?" questions after the read-aloud and model the short answers: "Yes, I am. / No, I'm not." Sample family line if stuck: "My mum is kind. My brothers are loud."
