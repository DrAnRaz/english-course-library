⚡ Warm-Up — Silly Challenges! 5 minStep 2

How to play:

1. Your teacher asks a silly question: "Can you touch your nose with your elbow?" 🤪
2. You TRY it — then answer: "No, I can't!" or "Yes, I can!"
3. Then YOU ask your teacher a silly "Can you…?" question.

No score here — just wake up your English brain! 🧠

Teacher note — Step 2

🌟 The big day

On Friday, our school has a talent show. My name is Aisha and I am very excited — and a little scared! The show is in the evening, in the school hall, and all our families come to watch.

💃 What I can do

I can sing and I can dance. I can also do a cartwheel, but I can't do two cartwheels — I fall down! My best friend Petra can juggle three balls. She can't juggle four. Nobody is perfect! We practise together in Petra's garden after school.

😗 My brother's trick

My little brother Samir can whistle very loudly. He can't sing at all, but he doesn't care. He whistles his favourite song and everybody laughs. Mum films him on her phone every time.

🍀 Wish us luck

Can we win first prize? I don't know. But we can try — and we can have fun! Our teacher says that fun is the real prize.

🔧 Grammar — Can / Can't 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

✅ CAN — one form for everybody!

can + verb (no "to", no -s!)

I can swim. 🏊
He can swim. (NOT "he cans"!)
My dog can catch a ball.

🔑 can + verb — nothing extra

❓ CAN'T + questions

can't = cannot. Question: flip the words!

I can't whistle. 😗
Can you swim? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.

🔑 Can + person + verb?
💡 The magic pattern: "I can sing, but I can't dance — yet!" The little word yet means: not now, but one day! 🌱

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian says "tudok úszni" — "I know to swim". In English there is NO "to" after can: "I can swim", never "I can to swim". And can never takes -s: "he can", not "he cans".
