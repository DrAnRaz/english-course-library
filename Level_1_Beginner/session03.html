🚪 Welcome to our classroom

Hi! I'm Amara. Our classroom is big and bright. It is my favourite place in school. The walls are yellow and the door is blue.

There is a large whiteboard at the front. There are two windows on the left. They are always open in summer. From the windows, we see the playground.

🪑 Desks and things

There are twenty desks in our room. Each student has a desk and a chair. The desks are old, but they are not broken.

I sit next to my best friend, Jon. I have a small pencil case with two pens and a ruler. Jon has a big green pencil case with stickers! He has three pencils too, but he has no rubber.

📚 The fun corner

There is a bookshelf at the back. It has dictionaries and art books. There is a big box of games too. We play them on rainy days.

Our teacher has a computer and a plant on her desk. The plant has red flowers.

On the walls, there are posters and maps. Every student has a picture on the wall! There is a big clock over the door. It is five minutes slow! I love our classroom. Do you like yours?

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: at the front · twenty · two pens and a ruler · red flowers. Open-question key: 1. any true sentence with there is (one thing) or there are (many things) — check the is/are match; 2. any list opening with "I have". After reading, ask: "When does Amara say THERE IS and when does she say SHE HAS?" — place vs person owning something. That contrast opens Step 5.
🔧 Grammar — have/has + there is/there are 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

Two super-useful tools today: have/has = a person OWNS something 🎒 · there is/are = something EXISTS in a place 🏫

✅ HAVE / HAS — owning

🟦 I / you / we / they → have
🟪 he / she / it → has

I have a blue pencil case.
Jon has a green one.
The plant has red flowers.

📍 THERE IS / THERE ARE — a place

there is + ONE thing 📋
there are + MORE THAN ONE 🪑🪑🪑

There is a whiteboard.
There are twenty desks.
💡 The magic question: is it about a PERSON owning something (have/has) or about WHAT IS IN a place (there is/are)?
"My teacher has a computer. There is a computer on her desk." — same computer, two tools!

💡 Hungarian tip: "Van egy tábla a teremben" = "There is a whiteboard in the classroom" — almost the same structure! This one is a WIN for Hungarian speakers. Just remember: many things → there ARE.

✏️ Practice — tap the correct word
