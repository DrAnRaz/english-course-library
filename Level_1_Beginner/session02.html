📹 Zoom: share screen. Student reads one chunk aloud, you read the next — take turns. Then the student annotates every verb ending in -s on your screen (Zoom → Annotate).

Read about Tomas's day. Tap 🔊 to hear any part read aloud.

🌅 Morning

Tomas is twelve years old. He lives in Leeds, in England.

He wakes up at seven o'clock. He gets dressed and has breakfast with his mum. He usually eats toast and drinks orange juice. His little brother Alfie drinks milk. Alfie is only four, so he stays at home.

🏫 School time

School starts at half past eight. Tomas walks to school with his friend Priya. They talk about football on the way. Priya likes maths, but Tomas likes English best.

At one o'clock, Tomas has lunch in the school canteen. He usually has a sandwich and an apple. Priya sits with him every day.

🌙 After school

School finishes at half past three. Tomas goes home and does his homework. Then he plays outside with Alfie in the garden.

In the evening, the family has dinner at six. After dinner, Tomas reads a book. He goes to bed at half past nine — never late! His mum says goodnight and turns off the light.

🎉 The weekend

On Saturdays, everything is different. Tomas wakes up at nine and plays football all afternoon. He loves weekends! On Sundays, the family visits Grandma. She makes a big lunch for everyone.

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: seven o'clock · toast and orange juice · at one o'clock · plays football. Open-question key: 1. any honest comparison with at least one "I + verb" sentence; 2. two full sentences with "at + time" ("I wake up at seven. I go to bed at ten."). After reading, hunt the -s verbs together (wakes, gets, has, eats, drinks, walks, goes, does, reads, loves) — that hunt IS the bridge to Step 5.
🔧 Grammar — Present Simple: the -s Rule 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

When we talk about what he or she does every day, the verb gets a little -s at the end. It is a tiny signal that says: "this is about someone else, not me!" 🔔

✅ I / YOU / WE / THEY — no change

I wake up at 7.
You go to school.
We have lunch.
They play football.

⚠️ HE / SHE / IT — add -s

She wakes up at 7.
He goes to school. (go → goes)
She watches TV. (watch → watches)
He has lunch. (have → has! ⚠️)
💡 The magic pattern: person + verb(+s) + at + time.
"Tomas wakes up at seven o'clock. I wake up at half past seven."

💡 Hungarian tip: in Hungarian the he/she form often has no extra ending (ő olvas), so the English -s is easy to forget. Train your ear: she readS, he goES. Say the S loudly while you practise!
