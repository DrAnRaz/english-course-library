⏳ The Time Machine Question — Starline School Magazine

Last week our science club built a cardboard "time machine" for the school fair — a painted box with three broken clocks and a bicycle wheel glued to the side. It doesn't actually work, obviously. But it made everyone in the corridor ask the same question: if you could travel in time, what would you change?

🎻 Nadia, 15 — the missed audition

"Two years ago, our music teacher invited me to audition for the county youth orchestra. I was terrified, so I pretended to be ill and stayed at home. I have regretted it ever since."

"If I had gone to that audition, I would probably be playing in the orchestra now. That is my biggest 'if only'. I wish I had been braver."

🎮 Kenji, 13 — no regrets allowed

Kenji sees it completely differently. "People waste too much time on regret," he says. "If I visited the past, I wouldn't change a single thing — one tiny change could break the whole timeline."

"Think about the famous grandparent paradox. If you stopped your grandparents from meeting, you would never have been born — so who travelled back to stop them? Exactly — my brain hurts."

🐕 Bianka, 14 — one small door

Bianka's answer surprised us the most. "I wouldn't visit the dinosaurs or ancient Rome. I would go back to one rainy Tuesday last spring, when I walked past the animal shelter and didn't go in."

"My neighbour adopted the scruffy little dog I saw in the window that day, and I still see him over the fence every single morning. He is a lovely dog — but if I had gone inside, he would be MY dog today. I still wish I lived with that fluffy disaster."

🔮 So… what would YOU change?

Here is what we learned from a broken cardboard box. Everyone — even "no regrets" Kenji — has a moment they think about rewriting. Nadia would take the chance she missed, and Bianka would simply open one small door.

Maybe that is the real magic of the time machine question. It is not really about the past at all — it quietly shows you what matters to you right now. Try asking it at dinner tonight, and see what your family says.

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: she pretended to be ill · nothing at all · you would never have been born · the dog would be Bianka's today. Open Q1 key: "If I had gone to that audition" = PAST (past perfect); "I would probably be playing now" = PRESENT result (would + be + -ing) — this sentence IS the mixed conditional, milk it before Step 5. Open Q2: accept any correct wish + past perfect and mixed conditional; content can be tiny ("I wish I had ordered the other pizza").
🔧 Grammar — Wishes, Regrets & Mixed Conditionals 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer BEFORE you click — and points ⬅️ (past) or ⬇️ (now) to show which time each half talks about.

😞 REGRETS about the PAST

I wish / if only + past perfect
I wish I had been braver.
If only I hadn't stayed at home!

3rd conditional — past → past:
If I had gone, I would have met the conductor.

🌀 MIXED — past cause → PRESENT result

If + past perfect, would + verb (now)
If I had gone to the audition, I would be in the orchestra now.
If she had adopted the dog, he would be hers today.

💭 Present wish: I wish + past simple
I wish I lived with that dog.
💡 The time-arrow trick: ask two questions. WHEN was the action? (past → had done). WHEN is the result? (past → would have been · now → would be). Mixed conditionals just point the arrow from the past at today.

💡 Hungarian tip: "bárcsak" + past ≈ I wish I had… for regrets. But careful: a wish about NOW uses PAST tense in English — "I wish I lived closer" (bárcsak közelebb laknék) — even though you mean the present. Don't translate word by word!

Teacher note — Step 5

6+/8 → bank time for Step 6. Under 5 → redo misses with the time-arrow question: "When did it happen? When is the result?" Most common trap: item 2 and 5 (mixed: "would be", not "would have been"). If they nail those two, they've got it.

Your time machine can deliver two letters: one to past you, one to future you. Fill in your time file first, then write! 📬

First, tap the time destinations you would visit:

😞 One real (small!) regret — use I wish I had… or If only I had…
🌀 How would TODAY be different? — mixed conditional
📨 Three lines to PAST you (advice, warning, or comfort)

Teacher note — Step 6

Keep regrets LIGHT (a missed party, a bad haircut, an unsaved game) — this is grammar practice, not therapy; redirect gently if something heavy comes up. Your follow-up question "how would today be different?" forces the mixed conditional in speech — that's the win condition of this task.
