📖 Reading — The Shark in the Lake 10 minStep 4
📹 Zoom: share screen, take turns reading one chunk each. Then student annotates every passive reporting phrase she finds (Zoom → Annotate).

Read the true-to-life story of a hoax that fooled a whole town. As you read, hunt for reporting phrases like was said to be. Tap 🔊 to hear any part.

🦈 The photo that started everything

On a rainy Tuesday last October, a photo appeared on a local news page in the small town of Milford. It showed a large grey fin cutting through the water of Lake Harmon. Within an hour, the headline "SHARK SPOTTED IN LAKE HARMON" had been shared more than ten thousand times.

📰 What the reports said

The shark was said to be over three metres long. It was reported that a fisherman had seen it "with his own eyes", and one website even claimed that a dog had been chased out of the water.

By Wednesday morning, schools near the lake were said to be planning to cancel their swimming lessons. Panic travels fast.

🕵️ Enter the fact-checkers

At Milford High School, a media club led by fourteen-year-old Priya Nair decided to investigate. The club had been trained to ask three simple questions: Who published this? What is the evidence? Do other reliable sources agree?

The answers were worrying. The "local news page" had been created only two weeks earlier, the fisherman was never named, and no other newspaper had reported the story at all.

🔍 The truth comes out

Then came the breakthrough. Using a reverse image search, the club discovered that the photo had actually been taken in Australia in 2019 — it is thought to have appeared in at least twelve fake stories since then.

And the "fin" in Lake Harmon? It was later confirmed to be a piece of black plastic pipe, placed in the water as a prank by two bored college students.

💭 Why it matters

The hoax was believed by thousands of people, partly because it felt exciting, and partly because everyone else was already sharing it. As Priya told a real journalist afterwards, "A lie doesn't need to be clever. It just needs to be shared faster than the truth."

Experts say that stories which trigger strong emotions — fear, anger, excitement — are shared far more often than calm, true ones. So next time a headline makes your heart race, pause. Ask who, ask what, ask where else. The shark you are afraid of might just be a plastic pipe.

🔧 Grammar — Passive Reporting & Headline Code 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

✅ IT + passive + THAT…

Report what "people in general" say — no source needed.

It is said that the lake is dangerous.
It was reported that a fisherman had seen it.
It is believed that thousands shared it.

🔑 Verbs that love this: say, report, believe, think, claim

🎯 SUBJECT + passive + TO-infinitive

Put the "star" of the story first — very news-style.

The shark is said to be 3 m long. (now)
The photo is thought to have come from Australia. (before now → to have + past participle!)

🔑 Same verbs, fancier melody 🎵
🗞️ Headline code: headlines drop a/the/is and squeeze time: present simple = a PAST event ("TEEN SAVES DOG" = a teen has saved a dog) · TO + verb = FUTURE ("MAYOR TO OPEN POOL" = the mayor is going to open a pool).

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian does all this with one little word — állítólag. English builds it INTO the verb instead: is said to…, is thought to…. When your brain says "állítólag", reach for a passive reporting verb.

✏️ Practice — tap the correct form

Teacher note — Step 5

The hardest jump is the perfect infinitive (is thought to have appeared) for past reference — items 3 and 8 test it. If those two fail, drill orally: "People say he left." → "He is said to HAVE LEFT." three times with different verbs. 6+/8 = speed up and bank time for Step 6.

Teacher note — Step 6

Push for both structures: one "It is believed that…" and one "X is said to be…". Sample (mystery smell): "A strange smell is said to be coming from the gym. It is believed that it started on Monday. The caretaker is thought to have found an old sandwich behind the lockers. The gym is reported to be open again tomorrow." Praise the clickbait version being deliberately terrible — that is the point.
