📹 Zoom: pure talking game, cameras on. Clap your hands once = the time machine sound. Use the 👍 reaction for every good echo.

How to play:

1. Your teacher says a TODAY sentence: "Today I walk to the shop."
2. You clap once 👏 (that's the time machine!) and send it to YESTERDAY: "Yesterday I walked to the shop!"
3. Five sentences, then swap roles — you say TODAY, the teacher time-travels.

No score here — just wake up your English brain! 🧠

Teacher note — Step 2

Use only regular verbs: walk, play, watch, listen, clean, help. Say the -ed ending clearly and slightly louder ("walkeD… playeD"). If the student echoes without -ed, repeat your sentence with a bigger clap — make the machine "fail" comically until the -ed appears.

📔 Dear Diary — by Zainab, 11

Yesterday was the strangest Saturday ever. In the morning, it was rainy and cold. I watched cartoons and listened to music in my pyjamas. Normal, right? Wait for it.

I texted my best friend and waited for the rain to stop. Boring? Just wait.

🍚 The kitchen disaster

At noon, my brother Danil cooked lunch for the first time in his life. Dad wasn't at home — he was at work.

The kitchen was a war zone! There was rice on the ceiling. Rice. On. The. Ceiling.

We cleaned the kitchen together for one hour, and Mum wasn't even angry — she just laughed.

Danil looked at the ceiling and whispered: "I only wanted to make a surprise." Well, he definitely surprised us!

🏡 The surprise visit

In the afternoon, my cousins visited us. We played board games and danced to silly songs.

My cousins loved the music. We jumped, we clapped, and Alma shouted all the words.

My little cousin Alma stopped in the middle of the dance and shouted: "The cat! Look at the cat!"

🐈 The famous cat

Our cat Mango was on the table — inside the empty rice pot. Her white fur was full of rice! We laughed for ten minutes.

Danil talked about it all evening. Later, I helped Alma with a puzzle, and we studied the photos of Mango again and again.

Mum snapped about twenty photos, and Danil posted the best one to the family chat. Everybody wanted a copy!

🌙 The end

It was a strange day, but the photos were fantastic. My parents were tired, but they were happy too. My weekend homework? I already have my story!

Before bed, I brushed my teeth, hugged Mango — very carefully! — and finished this diary page.

🔧 Grammar — Past Simple (-ed) + was / were 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer OUT LOUD before you click it.

✅ REGULAR VERBS — the -ed jacket

verb + -ed — same for I, you, he, she, we, they!

I played · she watched · we visited

✂️ Spelling tailors:
dance → danced (just +d)
study → studied (y → ied)
stop → stopped (double letter!)

⏰ WAS / WERE — past of "be"

I / he / she / it → was
you / we / they → were

It was rainy. My parents were tired.

❌ Negative: wasn't / weren't
Dad wasn't at home.
🔊 The three sounds of -ed — tap to hear: — only verbs ending in t/d get the extra "id" beat!

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian has ONE word for all of it — "volt". English past "be" changes with the person: I was, you were. Quick check every time: "Is it I/he/she/it (→ was) or you/we/they (→ were)?"

✏️ Practice — tap the correct form

If the student gets 6+/8, speed through and bank time for Step 6. If under 5, redo the wrong ones aloud with the person-check question ("who is the subject → was or were?"). Don't drill all three -ed sounds to perfection — recognition is enough at A2; production comes with exposure.
