📖 Reading — The Case of the Missing Sandwich 10 minStep 4
📹 Zoom: share screen. Read it like a radio drama — student is the narrator, you voice Mateo and Luna. Then the student annotates the quiz answers on your screen (Zoom → Annotate).

A detective story! Read it — tap 🔊 to hear any part read aloud.

🥪 One morning…

One Saturday morning, Mateo made the best sandwich of his life. Cheese, tomato, and three secret sauces.

He put it on the kitchen table and took a photo of it. Then he went upstairs to get his football shirt.

He also made a big glass of lemonade and put it next to the plate. A perfect Saturday lunch!

😱 Suddenly…

Two minutes later, he came back. The plate was empty. The sandwich was gone!

"Who ate my sandwich?" he shouted. His sister Luna said, "Not me! I drank a smoothie for breakfast."

Mateo looked at her for a long time. Luna looked back. "Really! I didn't eat it!" she said.

🕵️ The detective

Mateo got serious. He looked for clues, like a real detective.

Soon he found something small and yellow on the floor: a piece of cheese. Then he saw another piece near the sofa. The clues made a little cheese road across the room.

Mateo took another photo — a detective needs evidence! Then he followed the cheese road, step by step, very slowly.

🐶 The truth

The cheese road went behind the sofa. There, Mateo found Biscuit, the family dog.

Biscuit had a very happy face and a very round tummy. A piece of tomato sat on his nose.

😂 The end

"BISCUIT!" Mateo said. The dog ran to the garden at top speed. Everyone laughed — even Mateo.

In the end, Luna and Mateo made two new sandwiches together and ate them outside. Biscuit got nothing… but he still looked very, very proud.

Biscuit came back after five minutes with big, sorry eyes. He went straight to his basket and had a long nap. Detectives need rest — and so do sandwich thieves!

🔧 Grammar — Rebel Verbs & Story Words 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer OUT LOUD before you click it.

🔥 IRREGULAR = REBEL VERBS

No -ed! They change their whole shape:

go → went · eat → ate · see → saw
make → made · take → took · run → ran

🎵 Learn them in PAIRS, like song lyrics — there is no rule to save you!

❓ NEGATIVES & QUESTIONS — the rebel goes home

With did / didn't, use the BASE verb:

I didn't go. ✅ (not "didn't went" ❌)
Did you see it? ✅ (not "Did you saw" ❌)

🔑 "did" already carries the past — the verb relaxes!
📚 The story skeleton — every good story stands on these bones:
One day… → Suddenly… → Then… → In the end…
"One day Mateo made a sandwich. Suddenly it was gone! Then he found a cheese road. In the end, everyone laughed."

💡 Hungarian tip: in Hungarian, the past marker -t/-tt works on EVERY verb (ment, evett, látott). English rebels refuse the -ed jacket — you must memorize each pair. Good news: the 12 you learned today cover most everyday stories!
