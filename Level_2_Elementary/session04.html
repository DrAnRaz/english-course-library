📹 Zoom: pure camera game — teacher mimes on camera, student guesses. Use the 👍 reaction when the guess is right.

How to play:

1. Your teacher mimes an action on camera — no words! 🤫
2. You guess with a full sentence: "You are brushing your teeth!"
3. Then swap: YOU mime, the teacher guesses. 3 mimes each!

No score here — just wake up your English brain! 🧠

Teacher note — Step 2

Easy mimes to start: drinking tea, playing guitar, taking a photo. Insist gently on the full "You are …-ing" answer — echo it back correctly if it comes out short ("Dancing!" → "Yes! I AM DANCING!"). Big acting = big energy for the lesson.

🏠 Saturday morning live — by Niko, 12

Every Saturday morning, my family follows the same plan. Dad cooks pancakes and sings badly. Mum drinks two big cups of coffee and reads the news.

My little sister watches cartoons on the sofa. I usually sleep until ten o'clock. Our dog Pixel waits outside my door like a small, hairy alarm clock.

📢 But right now… listen!

Today is different! Right now, Dad is not cooking. He is dancing in the kitchen to his old rock music. He is using a spoon as a microphone.

Mum is laughing so hard that her coffee is shaking. She is taking a video on her phone.

📱 What is my sister doing?

My sister is not watching cartoons. She is standing on the sofa and clapping. She is planning to show the video to our cousins.

🐶 And Pixel?

Pixel is not waiting by my door any more. He is jumping around Dad's feet and barking at the music. He thinks this is the best game ever.

🛌 What about me?

Me? I am not sleeping — nobody can sleep in this house! I am sitting on the stairs and writing this on my phone.

I usually hate mornings, but I love this one. Right now, my crazy family is making my whole day.

🔎 Did you understand? Tap your answer

Teacher note — Step 4 + answer key

Quiz answers: cooks pancakes · dancing in the kitchen · taking a video on her phone · Pixel the dog. Open Q1 sample: "Because the house is very loud / his family is dancing and laughing." Open Q2: any two correct "is/are + -ing" sentences — accept pets and even objects ("The washing machine is working"). Notice the text flips tense at each subhead: the first block is "every Saturday" (simple), the rest is "right now" (continuous). Ask "where does the text change?" — that question IS the bridge to Step 5.
🔧 Grammar — Present Continuous vs Present Simple 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer OUT LOUD before you click it.

📸 PRESENT CONTINUOUS — "right now"

am / is / are + verb-ing
It is happening at this moment — like a photo of NOW.

Dad is dancing in the kitchen. 🕺
I am sitting on the stairs.

🔑 Signal words: now, right now, at the moment, Look!, Listen!

🔁 PRESENT SIMPLE — "every day"

verb (+ -s for he/she/it)
A habit or routine — it happens again and again.

Dad cooks pancakes every Saturday. 🥞
I usually sleep until ten.

🔑 Signal words: every day, usually, always, never, on Saturdays
💡 The reporter question: ask yourself — "Is it happening RIGHT NOW, or is it a HABIT?"
"Dad usually cooks… but right now he is dancing!"

💡 Hungarian tip: Hungarian uses the SAME verb form for both — "most táncol" and "minden nap táncol". English changes the verb: dances (habit) vs is dancing (now). So don't translate — ask the reporter question!

✏️ Practice — tap the correct form

Teacher note — Step 5
