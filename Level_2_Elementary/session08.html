🌞 My dream weekend plan — by Rafael, 12

On Friday my teacher asked us: "What are you going to do this weekend?" Most students said "nothing much". Not me! I have a big plan.

🎂 Saturday — cake day

On Saturday morning, I'm going to sleep late. No alarm clock! Then my dad and I are going to bake a chocolate cake. Dad says it's going to be a disaster. I believe in us.

In the afternoon, we're going to visit my grandparents. Grandma would like to see my new skateboard tricks. Grandpa says he would like to try the skateboard too. Mum says no. Loudly.

🧺 Sunday — picnic day

On Sunday, my friends and I are going to have a picnic by the lake. Žaneta is going to bring her dog, Biscuit. Biscuit is going to steal our sandwiches. He always does.

We're going to bring extra sandwiches this time. We have a system now.

🌙 The evening

In the evening, I'd like to watch a film, but I'm not going to stay up late. School starts on Monday. Ugh.

So that's my dream weekend: cake, grandparents, a picnic and a sandwich-stealing dog. What about you? What are you going to do this weekend?

🔧 Grammar — Going To vs Would Like To 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

✅ GOING TO — the plan (decided!)

am / is / are + going to + verb

I'm going to bake a cake. 🎂
She isn't going to stay up late.
Are you going to come?

🔑 Also for things you can SEE coming: "Black clouds! It's going to rain." 🌧️

💭 WOULD LIKE TO — the wish (a dream)

would like to + verb (short: 'd like to)

I'd like to visit Japan one day. 🗾
Would you like to try?

🔑 It's the polite dreamer: not decided, just wanted.
💡 The quick test: Is it in your calendar? → going to. Is it in your dreams? → would like to.
"On Saturday I'm going to visit Grandma. One day I'd like to visit the Moon." 🌕

💡 Hungarian tip: "fogok" ≈ going to, "szeretnék" ≈ would like to. Two traps: English needs am/is/are before going to, and would like needs TO — never "I would like watch".

Push for time phrases: "in the morning / then / after that". Follow-ups: "Who is going to come with you? What are you going to eat?" Each question forces another going-to sentence. End with "Would you like to do this weekend for real?"
