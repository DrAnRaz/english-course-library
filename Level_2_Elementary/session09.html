⚡ Warm-Up — Rule or Advice? 5 minStep 2
📹 Zoom: cameras on, hands visible! Student shows fingers to answer — no clicking, no screen yet.

How to play:

1. Your teacher says a sentence — for example: "Stop at a red light!" 🚦
2. You show 1 finger if it's a RULE (must!) — or 2 fingers if it's just ADVICE (should).
3. Then say it as a full sentence: "You must stop at a red light." Then swap roles! 🔄

No score here — just wake up your English brain! 🧠

🏕️ Welcome to Camp Falcon! — a letter from Dalia

Hi! My name is Dalia. Last year I was a camper here. This year I am a junior helper. Here is everything you must know — plus some friendly advice from me.

📜 The rules — you must follow these

You must wear a helmet on the climbing wall. No helmet, no climbing. That is the biggest rule at camp.

You have to be at breakfast at eight o'clock. Mr Okafor, the cook, closes the kitchen at half past eight — and he never waits.

You also have to keep your cabin tidy. Every morning a helper checks the cabins, and the tidiest cabin wins a star.

You mustn't feed the squirrels. They look cute, but they are little thieves. Last year one took my whole sandwich!

And you mustn't leave the camp without a leader. The forest is big, and all the paths look the same.

😊 The good news

You don't have to make a campfire alone — a helper always does it with you. And you don't have to swim if you don't want to. Nobody has to do everything.

You don't have to bring food, because Mr Okafor cooks everything — and his pancakes are the best in the world.

💡 My advice

You should bring a torch, because the path to the cabins is very dark at night. You should write your name on your socks. Trust me on this one.

You should also drink lots of water on hot days, and you should ask the helpers anything — we love questions.

And you shouldn't leave chocolate in your bag. Remember the squirrels? See you at Camp Falcon — I can't wait to meet you! 🦅

🔧 Grammar — Must / Have To / Should 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, the student says the answer out loud BEFORE you click it.

📜 MUST / HAVE TO — strong rules

must + verb · have/has to + verb

You must wear a helmet. ⛑️
She has to be there at eight.

🔑 No "to" after must: must wear, NOT "must to wear"!

💡 SHOULD — friendly advice

should / shouldn't + verb

You should bring a torch. 🔦
You shouldn't leave chocolate in your bag. 🍫

🔑 It's a good idea — not a rule.
⚠️ The danger pair — NOT the same!
mustn't = ❌ forbidden! "You mustn't feed the squirrels."
don't have to = 🆗 not necessary, you choose. "You don't have to swim."

💡 Hungarian tip: "kell" = must / have to, "nem szabad" = mustn't, "nem kell" = don't have to. Hungarian "nem kell" and "nem szabad" look like cousins — in English they are TOTAL strangers. Never swap them!
