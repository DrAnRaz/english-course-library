🏰 The Golden Key of Greystone

Last month, four friends visited Greystone Castle: Tarek, Juno, Bea and Sam. People say it is the oldest castle in the country — and that a golden key is hidden inside it.

🚪 The locked door

The friends walked across the old bridge and found a huge wooden door. There was a sign on it: "You must answer three questions to enter."

Sam read the questions aloud, and Juno knew every answer. She was the smartest of the four. The door opened slowly, with a sound like an old dinosaur.

🦉 The dark tower

Inside, the castle was darker than a cave. Suddenly, they heard a strange sound above them. "Something is moving up there!" whispered Bea.

It wasn't a ghost. It was an old owl — and it was angrier than a wet cat.

🗝️ The golden key

At the top of the tower, Tarek saw a small wooden box. Inside was the golden key — and a note: "Well done, adventurers. Next year, we are going to hide the key again. You should come back!"

The friends took a photo with the key and walked home in the sunshine. Everyone agreed: it was the best day of the spring.

⏳ TIME MACHINE — the tenses

NOW (always): I play football on Mondays.
RIGHT NOW: I am playing right now!
YESTERDAY: I played · I went · it was fun.
PLAN: I'm going to play tomorrow.

🧰 POWER TOOLS

Compare: bigger than · the biggest 🏆
Wish: I'd like to see it. 💭
Rules: must · mustn't · should 📜
Places: There is a castle. There are two towers. 🏰
💡 The adventurer's check: before you speak, ask — WHEN is it happening (now / right now / past / future)? And is it a fact, a comparison, a wish or a rule? Pick the right tool, then go! ⚔️
