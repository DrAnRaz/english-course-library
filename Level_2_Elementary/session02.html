🌍 Incredible animals

Our planet is full of amazing animals. Here are four of my favourites — and their superpowers!

🐬 Dolphins

Dolphins live in the ocean, but they are not fish — they are mammals. They can swim at 60 kilometres per hour, and they can jump high out of the water. They can even talk to each other with clicks and whistles!

But dolphins can't breathe underwater. They swim to the surface and breathe through a hole on the top of their heads.

Baby dolphins can't hunt at first, so they stay close to their mothers and learn from them every day.

🦅 Eagles

Eagles can fly higher than mountains. They have amazing eyes — an eagle can see a small mouse from 500 metres away! Eagles can't run fast on the ground, but they don't need to.

Eagles can build giant nests, too. Some nests are bigger than your bed, and an eagle family can use the same nest for many years!

🐆 Cheetahs

Cheetahs are the fastest land animals in the world. They can run at 120 kilometres per hour — faster than a car on a normal road! Cheetahs can't roar like lions, but they can purr like a happy cat.

But a cheetah can't run for a long time. After about thirty seconds it needs a rest — just like you after a big race!

🐧 Penguins

Penguins are birds, but they can't fly. They can swim brilliantly — they use their wings like flippers. A penguin can hold its breath for 20 minutes and can survive at minus 60 degrees!

On land, penguins can't walk very fast. So they lie down and slide on the ice on their tummies — it looks like a fun game!

🔧 Grammar — can / can't for Ability 10 minStep 5
📹 Zoom: share screen. For each quiz item, student says the answer BEFORE you click it.

✅ CAN — able to do it

Dolphins can swim fast. 🐬
Eagles can fly high.
She can swim. ← NO -s, ever!

🔑 can + base verb: can swim, can fly — never "can swims", never "can to swim"

❌ CAN'T — not able

Penguins can't fly. 🐧
Butterflies can't swim.
I can't fly! 😄

❓ Question: flip the order! Can dolphins jump? — Yes, they can. / No, they can't.
💡 "Can" is a magic helper word: it never changes. No -s, no -ed, no -ing. One shape for everyone: I can, you can, she can, they can. 🪄
🌍 Where does it live? Say the habitat: It lives in the ocean. 🐬 · Its home is the forest. 🐺 — use these two lines for your fact card in Step 6!

💡 Hungarian tip: "tud úszni" = "can swim" — the idea is the same! The ONE difference: in English questions the order flips: "Can it fly?", not "It can fly?". Watch only for that.
